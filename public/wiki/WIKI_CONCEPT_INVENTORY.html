PART I: THE ADMISSION RUBRIC

Auto-pass criteria (any one is sufficient)

CodeCriterionDefinition
DCDistinct YAML CoordinateHas a unique signature in the geometry (specific position, dimension member, ring number)
LRLint-RelevantA structural invariant references it (lint cannot operate without it)
OAOriginal 1991 Derivation AnchorNamed in the 1991 transmission documents OR foundational architecture documents predating 2024 corpus consolidation

Discretionary criteria (need 2-of-4, with at least one being LB or FN)

CodeCriterionDefinition
LBLoad-BearingRemoving it breaks the derivation of something else
CRCross-ReferencedInvoked by name in 3+ corpus documents OR 2+ other concept nodes
ADAtomically DefinableCan be stated in 50–200 words without recursing back to itself
FNFoundationalCannot be derived from prior concepts in the framework

Exclusion rules

  • Phrase variants of an existing concept → alias, not new node
  • Instances/examples of a more general concept → general concept is the node
  • Single-doc appearances with no outside dependencies → live where authored
  • Pure voice/narrative content → corpus stays canonical for voice
  • Legacy renames (Card, Correction, House-as-Practice, Channel-as-Activity, Physical-House) → not nodes; documented in TERMINOLOGY_RENAME_TRACKER

PART VI: WHAT IS DELIBERATELY OUT OF SCOPE

These show up in the corpus but do NOT merit wiki nodes:

  • Legacy renamed terms: “Card” (now Signature), “Correction” (now Rebalancing), “House” (now Practice — already have node under new name), “Channel” (now Activity), “Physical House” (now Body). All documented in TERMINOLOGY_RENAME_TRACKER.md.
  • Product-side concepts: Logic Gate, Glistener, Reader UI features. These belong to the Reader application, not the framework.
  • Personal narratives: Chris’s biographical content (narratives/personal/), individual session transcripts. Stay corpus-only.
  • Phenomenological depth: Deep voice/narrative on each archetype’s lived character. Stays corpus-canonical; wiki nodes reference via Pointer Node pattern.
  • Single-doc concepts with no outside dependencies: many one-off ideas in derivation files that didn’t propagate. Live where they were authored.

PART VII: OPEN QUESTIONS FOR REVIEW

Before any node authoring begins, these need Chris+Gemini decisions:

  1. Borderlines (Part IV) — eight concepts to promote, decline, or merge.
  2. Pointer Node pattern confirmed? — wiki nodes link to specific corpus sections (e.g., [[Ring_System_Complete#emotion-practice|emotion-practice]]) rather than fabricating new per-archetype phenomenology files. Confirm.
  3. Confidence tiers as YAML field? — should every node carry tier: DERIVED|STRONGLY_CONSTRAINED|PRINCIPLED|VECTOR|VALIDATED|CORRESPONDENCE? My read: yes. Confirm.
  4. Naming convention for files? — propose Type_Name.md (e.g., Principle_The_Veil.md, Archetype_06_Compassion.md, Bound_04_Cups_Reverie.md, Ring_04_Emotion.md). Confirm or revise.
  5. Sprint sequencing? — Tier 1 first as proposed, or do you want a different order (e.g., do all 78 positions first because they’re auto-pass and need no deliberation)?
  6. Lint priorities? — which invariants does the lint script check first? My proposed order: vertical-sum-20 (cleanest test), then bound-sum-11, then governance edges, then MOLS, then 22-step closure.
